Privacy Policy

glas.sh is built on the principle that your terminal sessions are yours. We don't collect, transmit, or monetize your data. Everything this policy describes is what the app technically does — not a promise we hope to keep.

Last updated: May 17, 2026  ·  Effective: v1.0.0

Summary

  • We don't collect personal information.
  • We don't run analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, or any first-party tracking.
  • The app contains no third-party SDKs that phone home.
  • All your SSH credentials, server configurations, session recordings, and AI assistant interactions stay on your device.
  • When you connect to a remote server, we send traffic only to that server — over SSH, that you initiated.

What stays on your device

  • SSH server configurations — hostnames, ports, usernames, tags, jump-host relationships. Stored in UserDefaults inside a shared App Group accessible only to apps you've installed from us (currently glas.sh and glassdb).
  • Passwords and SSH private keys — stored in iOS / visionOS Keychain with accessibility level kSecAttrAccessibleWhenUnlockedThisDeviceOnly. Never synced to iCloud. Never leave your device.
  • Secure Enclave-protected keys — when enabled, your SSH key material is encrypted at rest with a P-256 keypair that lives in the Apple Secure Enclave and never leaves it. Retrieval requires biometric or device-passcode authentication.
  • Session recordings — when you record a terminal session, the recording is written to the app's sandboxed container on your device.
  • AI command assistant interactions — your prompts and the AI's responses are processed entirely on-device using Apple's Foundation Models framework. No prompts, responses, or terminal contents are sent to any cloud AI service.
  • App preferences and layouts — terminal themes, window arrangements, font sizes, and other personalization stay in local UserDefaults.

What leaves your device, when, and to whom

  • SSH traffic. When you connect to a server, encrypted SSH traffic flows between your device and the server you specified. We aren't a man-in-the-middle. We don't proxy, log, or relay your sessions through any infrastructure of ours.
  • Tailscale device discovery. If you opt in to Tailscale integration, the app makes API calls directly from your device to api.tailscale.com using credentials you provide (API key or OAuth client). These credentials are stored in Keychain and used only to fetch your tailnet's device list. We have no relationship with Tailscale; the connection is between your device and Tailscale's API.
  • SharePlay. If you start a SharePlay session to share a terminal view with someone in a FaceTime call, terminal video frames are transmitted through Apple's Group Activities infrastructure. This uses Apple's services, not ours, and is governed by Apple's privacy policy. The viewer receives a read-only stream.

Permissions we request

  • Network access. Required to make SSH connections to the servers you specify.
  • Keychain access. Required to securely store SSH credentials.
  • Local Authentication (Face ID / Optic ID / passcode). Required only if you enable Secure Enclave–protected SSH keys, to authorize each use of a protected key.
  • Group Activities. Required only if you start a SharePlay session.

The app does not request access to your contacts, location, photos, microphone, or camera. We don't ask for tracking permission because there's nothing to track.

Children

glas.sh is a developer and system-administration tool. It is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect any information from children.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change this policy, the change will appear in the version control history of this page (on GitHub) and the "Last updated" date at the top will move. We will not retroactively start collecting or transmitting data that this policy says we don't.
